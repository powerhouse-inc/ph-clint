# Messaging & Site Outline - Skill Preamble

With this skill, you draft, iterate, and finalize the site messaging. This is a content exercise, NOT a design exercise. You decide WHAT to say, not HOW it looks.

## Interaction Mode

**expert mode**: Collaborate with the user on messaging decisions. Present drafts for review and incorporate feedback precisely. Keep the iteration loop tight.

**discovery mode**: Explain messaging strategy concepts (narrative arc, funnel distribution, CTA placement) as you go. Walk through each section's purpose.

**one-shot mode**: Draft the complete site outline autonomously and present for review. Still require explicit approval before finalizing.

## Critical Guards

### 1. Lead With Audience Concerns, Not Product Features
The hero section must address what keeps the audience up at night. The product is the answer, not the opening. If your first section heading mentions the product name, rewrite it.

### 2. Include a Changelog With Every Version
Every iteration is saved as `messaging/v{N}.md` with a changelog at the top: what changed, why, and what prompted the change. This prevents regression and creates an audit trail.

### 3. Concise Copy
Enterprise decision-makers scan. Every sentence must earn its place. If you can say it in one sentence, don't use three. If a section doesn't serve the narrative arc, cut it.

### 4. Don't Overclaim Maturity
If the product is early-stage, say "frontier technology" not "proven at scale." If there are no external clients, don't have a "Trusted By" section. Honest language about where the product is builds more trust than inflated claims.

### 5. Don't Duplicate Partner Site Content
If the product has ecosystem sites, the main site should funnel TO them, not reproduce their content. Each site has a job — don't blur the boundaries.

### 6. Funnel Links Throughout
CTAs appear throughout the content, not just at the bottom. Every section that establishes a concern should offer a path to resolution. The reader should never scroll more than 2 sections without seeing an actionable next step.

### 7. Document What Changed
Between versions, explicitly list: sections added, sections removed, sections rewritten, and WHY for each change. This tracks messaging convergence.

## Messaging Outputs

All outputs go to the `messaging/` directory:
- `v1.md` through `v{N}.md` — Full iteration history with changelogs
- `approved.md` — Final approved messaging (copy of approved version)
