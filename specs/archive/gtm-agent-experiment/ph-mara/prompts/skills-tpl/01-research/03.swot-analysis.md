# RS.03 SWOT Analysis

Prerequisite: Audience, product analysis, and emotional drivers complete.

## RS.03.1 Strengths — specific and defensible

- What's genuinely strong? Not "great team" but specific architectural, strategic, or market advantages
- For each strength: can a competitor replicate this within 12 months?
- For each strength: does the audience care about this? (A strength they don't value is trivia, not positioning)

## RS.03.2 Weaknesses — honest and actionable

- What's honestly weak? State the gap and its impact on the audience.
- Score severity: how much does this weakness affect the audience's decision?
- For each weakness: what's the honest framing? (e.g., "small team" → "direct access to builders")
- Do NOT minimize — a known weakness framed honestly is less damaging than a hidden one discovered later

## RS.03.3 Opportunities — external trends

- What market shifts, regulatory changes, or technology trends favor the product?
- Be specific: name the regulation, the market movement, the competitor misstep
- For each opportunity: what's the window? Is this urgent or developing?

## RS.03.4 Threats — including inertia

- The biggest competitor is usually "do nothing" / status quo inertia. Include it explicitly.
- Incumbent vendors embedding similar capabilities into existing platforms
- Direct competitors in the same space
- Enterprise sales cycle length as a threat to runway

## RS.03.5 Strategic implications

- What does the SWOT tell you about messaging priorities?
- What should the site lead with? (Usually the intersection of strength + opportunity)
- What should the site honestly address? (Weaknesses the audience will notice anyway)
- What should the site avoid claiming? (Strengths that are really aspirational)

### Expected Scenario Outcome

`research/swot.md` exists with specific, defensible strengths; honest weaknesses; concrete opportunities; and realistic threats including inertia. Strategic implications documented.
