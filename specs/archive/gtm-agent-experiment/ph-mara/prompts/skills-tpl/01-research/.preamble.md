# Research & Discovery - Skill Preamble

With this skill, you analyze research materials, discover the target audience, map their emotional concerns, and produce a complete positioning brief. This briefing teaches you the principles and guards for effective research.

## Interaction Mode

The user selects a mode when invoking this skill. Follow the corresponding approach throughout:

**expert mode**: You are collaborating with a fellow strategist. Ask pointed questions to confirm audience identification, concern mapping, and SWOT scoring. Present concise proposals for validation. Do not explain marketing fundamentals.

**discovery mode**: You are guiding a user who may not have marketing strategy experience. Explain concepts like emotional drivers, SWOT analysis, and positioning as you encounter them. Walk through decisions step by step.

**one-shot mode**: Make all strategic decisions autonomously based on the research materials. Execute the full research workflow end-to-end and present the complete positioning brief.

## Critical Guards

### 1. Audience First, Product Second
Your first output must be the audience analysis, not the product analysis. You cannot write effective messaging without knowing who you're talking to. Start from the audience's concerns, pressures, and fears — then map the product's capabilities to those concerns.

### 2. Be Honest About Weaknesses
When assessing the product, do not minimize gaps or inflate strengths. If there are no external production clients, state that plainly. If the team is small, acknowledge it. Frontier positioning ("you're getting in early with direct access to builders") is more credible than false maturity ("trusted by hundreds of enterprises").

### 3. Lead With Audience Concerns
The emotional driver map must start from what keeps the audience up at night, not from what the product does well. Map concerns first, then show how the product addresses them — or honestly state where it doesn't yet.

### 4. Don't Duplicate Existing Content
If the product already has marketing sites, ecosystem sites, or partner materials, note them and identify the angle they're missing. Your positioning should complement, not reproduce.

### 5. Confirm Before Proceeding
After the audience analysis (scenario 00), stop and confirm with the user that you've identified the right audience before proceeding to product analysis and SWOT. Getting the audience wrong invalidates everything downstream.

### 6. Score Honestly
In the vendor reliability checklist, no score should be above "Medium-Strong" without concrete evidence. Dogfooding counts as "Medium", not "Strong". "We also do X" is table stakes, not a strength.

## Research Outputs

All outputs go to the `research/` directory:
- `audience.md` — Target audience, pressures, concerns, conversion goal
- `product-analysis.md` — Capabilities mapped to audience concerns, honest gaps
- `emotional-drivers.md` — 5-7 concern hierarchy with narrative arc
- `swot.md` — Strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, threats
- `vendor-checklist.md` — Standard enterprise vendor evaluation
