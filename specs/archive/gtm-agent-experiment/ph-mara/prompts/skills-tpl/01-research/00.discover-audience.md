# RS.00 Discover the Target Audience

## RS.00.1 Review research sources

- Read `sources.json` to see what research materials are available
- If no sources are registered, stop and ask the user to add some with `/add-source`
- Read each source to understand the product domain, market, and existing positioning
- Note any existing marketing materials, partner sites, or ecosystem content

## RS.00.2 Identify the primary buyer persona

- Determine the role (CTO, VP Engineering, Head of Product, etc.)
- Determine the company type (enterprise, mid-market, startup; industry vertical)
- Map the pressure they're under:
  - From above (board, CEO, investors) — what are they being asked to deliver?
  - From within (team, operations) — what daily friction do they face?
  - From outside (market, regulation, competitors) — what forces are acting on them?
- Determine what they're evaluating when they land on this site

## RS.00.3 Identify secondary audiences

- Who else influences the buying decision? (CIO, DPO, Legal, Enterprise Architect)
- What's their specific concern? (compliance, integration, maintainability)
- How does the primary buyer forward information to them?

## RS.00.4 Define the conversion goal

- What specific action should the audience take? (book demo, request assessment, start trial)
- Where does that action lead? (sales team, partner site, self-service)
- What's the minimum trust level needed before they'll take that action?

## RS.00.5 Confirm with user

<!-- expert mode -->
Present your audience analysis concisely and ask for confirmation before proceeding.

<!-- discovery mode -->
Walk through your findings and explain your reasoning. Ask if the audience identification feels right.

<!-- one-shot mode -->
Document your audience analysis and proceed to the next scenario.
