# RS.04 Vendor Reliability Checklist

Prerequisite: SWOT analysis complete.

## RS.04.1 Evaluate against standard enterprise vendor questions

Score each honestly on: Weak / Weak-Medium / Medium / Medium-Strong / Strong

1. **Proven in production?** — Are there external production clients? Dogfooding counts as Medium at best.
2. **Will the company exist in 3 years?** — Funding, revenue, open source as insurance
3. **Can I hire people who know this?** — Mainstream stack vs. exotic technology
4. **Documentation and learning material?** — Quality, completeness, freshness
5. **Support model?** — SLAs, response times, escalation paths
6. **Is the open source healthy?** — Community size, contribution frequency, governance
7. **Integration with existing systems?** — Standards-based vs. bespoke integration work
8. **Migration path?** — Data portability, parallel adoption vs. rip-and-replace
9. **Reference architectures?** — Industry-specific examples, case studies
10. **Operational support (2am call)?** — Architecture resilience, human SLAs

## RS.04.2 Frame each gap honestly

- For every score below Medium-Strong: explain the honest framing
- What compensates for the gap? (e.g., "no production clients" → "demo shows real system, open source means no lock-in")
- What's the credible alternative the audience should hear? (Not "trust us" but "here's the evidence")

## RS.04.3 Identify the overarching framing

- What's the one-sentence positioning that addresses the overall score pattern?
- Is this "proven enterprise solution" (scores mostly Strong) or "frontier technology with co-creation partnership" (scores mostly Medium)?
- The framing must be honest — don't position as proven when scores say frontier

### Expected Scenario Outcome

`research/vendor-checklist.md` exists with 10-point evaluation, honest scores, gap framing, and overarching positioning statement.
