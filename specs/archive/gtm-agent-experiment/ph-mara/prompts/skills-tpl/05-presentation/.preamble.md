# Presentation Deck - Skill Preamble

With this skill, you produce a slide deck for presenting the product to the target audience. The deck follows the emotional driver arc from the research phase.

## Interaction Mode

**expert mode**: Review the narrative arc mapping before producing slides. Discuss slide count, content density, and visual approach.

**discovery mode**: Explain how emotional drivers map to slides and why the narrative arc matters for persuasion.

**one-shot mode**: Produce the complete deck autonomously following the emotional driver arc.

## Critical Guards

### 1. No Fake Metrics
NEVER include statistics, percentages, or quantitative claims that aren't sourced from real, verifiable data. "Saves time" is acceptable. "Saves 40% of time" is not (unless you can cite the source). This is the most important guard — fake metrics destroy credibility with technical audiences.

### 2. Forwardable Slides
Every slide must be understandable without a presenter. Include enough context that if someone forwards the deck, each slide communicates its point independently. No "as I mentioned" or "as we discussed" references.

### 3. Follow the Emotional Driver Arc
Slide order follows the audience's concern hierarchy from `research/emotional-drivers.md`. Don't reorganize for "logical flow" — the emotional flow IS the logical flow for persuasion.

### 4. Concise Per Slide
Maximum 6 bullet points per slide. Maximum 10 words per bullet. If a concept needs more, split into two slides or use speaker notes.

### 5. Screenshots, Not Live Demos
Don't assume live demo availability. Include screenshots from `site/` or product screenshots. Every visual must be a static file in the deck.

### 6. Design Consistency
Use the same color palette, typography, and tone as the site (`design-system/`). The deck should feel like it belongs to the same brand.

## Presentation Outputs

All outputs go to `deck/`:
- `outline.md` — Slide outline with narrative arc mapping
- `index.html` — Navigable HTML slide deck
- `css/deck.css` — Slide-specific styles
- `assets/screenshots/` — Product screenshots
