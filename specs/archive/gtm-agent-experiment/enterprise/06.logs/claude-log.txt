  1. Hero Section

  Headline: "Own Your Business Infrastructure. Make It AI-Native."
  Subhead: Enterprise-grade document architecture that keeps your data,
  processes, and intelligence in-house вҖ” while making them accessible to AI
  agents and automation.
  CTA: "Talk to Us" / "See How It Works"

  Enterprise translation of: "local-first, decentralized operating system for an
   AI-powered world"

  2. The Problem

  "Your Systems Weren't Built for AI"
  - Business logic locked in opaque ERP modules and proprietary formats
  - Process changes require expensive vendor engagements
  - AI adoption forces a false choice: hand data to cloud vendors or bolt
  chatbots onto legacy systems
  - Operational knowledge lives in tribal know-how, not machine-readable
  structures

  Adapts BAI's "most AI implementations fail because they skip the data layer" 
  framing, broadened beyond just AI to general digital transformation.

  3. The Approach вҖ” Three concise panels

  "Data Layer First. Then Intelligence."

  a) Structure вҖ” Model your business processes as typed, versioned documents
  with built-in audit trails. Every change is signed, timestamped, and
  append-only. Your data becomes AI-readable without leaving your
  infrastructure.

  b) Automate вҖ” Generate operational interfaces (CLI, chat, API, MCP) directly
  from your data models. AI agents and human operators work on the same
  structured data through the same typed operations. No custom integration per
  channel.

  c) Scale вҖ” Reactive document architecture built on proven patterns (event
  sourcing, CQRS). Scales from a department pilot to millions of users.
  Storage-agnostic: on-premise databases, cloud, or hybrid.

  ---
  2. Core Concept: Documents as Data Primitives

  "One data abstraction. Every business object."

  A Powerhouse document is a typed, self-contained data structure defined by a
  document model вҖ” a schema that specifies its shape and the exact operations
  that can modify it. Under the hood, it's JSON. On the wire, it's an
  append-only sequence of signed operations.

  What this gives you:

  - Schema-enforced consistency вҖ” Operations have well-defined parameters and
  built-in business logic. The document state is always valid by construction,
  not by validation after the fact.
  - Cryptographic verification вҖ” Every operation is signed by its author. The
  full history is offline-verifiable without trusting a central authority.
  - Git-like version control вҖ” Branching, merging, undo, and history traversal
  come free from the append-only model. No additional development needed.
  - Storage-agnostic persistence вҖ” The same document can live as a file, in a
  browser (pglite), in PostgreSQL, or on-chain. Append-only histories work in
  both mutable and immutable repositories.
  - AI-native by default вҖ” Every document model automatically generates GraphQL
  and MCP endpoints. The same operations available to human editors are
  available to AI agents and integration workflows вҖ” no adapter layer.

  This section establishes architectural credibility. A CTO reading this should 
  think: "These people understand event sourcing and have built something 
  principled on top of it."

  ---
  3. Architecture: Reactive Document Infrastructure

  "Event-sourced. CQRS. Horizontally scalable. Local-first."

  The reactive document architecture is the infrastructure layer that turns
  documents from individual files into a platform-grade backend.

  Write path вҖ” All mutations are document operations. Because documents are
  self-contained and independent, the write layer scales horizontally by adding
  nodes. There's no shared-state bottleneck; each document is its own
  consistency boundary.

  Read path вҖ” Specialized processors subscribe to document update events and
  build denormalized read models. Store aggregated data wherever it fits:
  relational databases, search indexes, data warehouses, caches. Read and write
  concerns are fully separated (CQRS).

  Sync layer вҖ” Documents synchronize across devices, apps, and users via their
  operation histories. This turns every document into a real-time collaboration
  channel and every editor into a collaborative application вҖ” without custom
  sync code.

  Result: The same architecture pattern that powers large-scale platforms
  (millions of users, real-time, eventually consistent) вҖ” but local-first, open
  source, and running on your infrastructure.

  No analogies to products they might already use. Just the patterns, clearly 
  stated. An enterprise architect can evaluate this against their existing 
  event-driven infrastructure.

  4. The Platform

  "Six components. Each independent. Stronger together."

  Connect

  The document management workspace. Operators create, edit, and collaborate on
  business documents through a browser-based UI. Drive apps overlay the file
  system with specialized workflow interfaces вҖ” standardized best-practice
  processes directly available, rather than leaving operators to organize their
  own folder hierarchies.

  Switchboard

  The API and integration layer. Exposes every document and operation as GraphQL
   queries/mutations and MCP tool endpoints. This is the system boundary where
  external applications, automation workflows, and AI agents interact with your
  document infrastructure. Handles authorization, sync, and multi-tenant access.

  Clint

  AI-native command infrastructure. A single set of Zod-based command
  definitions вҖ” derived directly from your document models вҖ” produces a CLI
  (command mode and interactive REPL), an MCP server, and an AI agent harness.
  Operators use typed commands or natural language; autonomous agents use the
  same operations through the routine loop вҖ” a tick-based execution engine with
  pluggable triggers for event-driven, timer-based, or condition-based
  processing. One codebase, every interaction surface.

  Fusion

  Custom platform builder. A Next.js-based framework for deploying branded,
  customer-facing applications on top of your document infrastructure.
  Switchboard-backed, fully customizable. Build the equivalent of a SaaS product
   вҖ” with the backend entirely in your hands.

  Renown

  Identity and reputation layer. Verified contributor profiles and contribution
  tracking across your organization and partner networks. Provides the identity
  substrate for authorization, audit trails, and cross-organizational
  collaboration.

  Vetra

  The platform ecosystem. Three pillars:
  - Packages вҖ” A registry of reusable document models, editors, read models, and
   agent configurations. Use pre-built modules for common business domains or
  publish your own.
  - Hosting вҖ” Managed cloud infrastructure with real-time sync. Production-ready
   deployment without hyperscaler dependency. Your data sovereignty is
  preserved.
  - AI Agents вҖ” Pre-configured business process agents built on the document
  architecture. Deploy from the ecosystem or build custom agents with Clint.

  Development tooling (Vetra Studio IDE, PH-CLI) and a learning platform (Vetra
  Academy) support teams building on the stack.

  Each component gets enough depth for a CTO to understand its role and evaluate
   whether it replaces or complements something in their current architecture. 
  Clint stands alongside the others as a peer, not an add-on.

  5. AI Integration Model

  "Native, Not Bolted On"

  Most enterprise AI integrations require building a translation layer between
  the AI and the business system. Powerhouse eliminates this by making documents
   the shared interface for humans and machines.

  How it works:
  - Document models generate MCP endpoints automatically. Any MCP-compatible AI
  agent can read and write business data through the same typed operations that
  human editors use.
  - Clint's agent skills are structured process playbooks (not open-ended
  prompts) that encode domain expertise as step-by-step workflows. Agents follow
   specifications, not guesses.
  - The routine loop enables autonomous processing: agents monitor document
  changes, evaluate triggers, and execute operations on a continuous cycle вҖ”
  with or without human oversight.
  - Provider-agnostic: Sensitive workflows run on local open-source models (your
   infrastructure, your GPU). General-purpose tasks use commercial APIs. Swap
  providers without architectural changes.

  What stays in your control:
  - All data вҖ” documents never leave your infrastructure for model training
  - All operations вҖ” every AI action is a signed document operation in the
  append-only history
  - All logic вҖ” business rules live in document model reducers, not in prompt
  engineering

  A CTO evaluating AI strategy needs to know: how does AI plug in, what does it 
  have access to, and what guardrails exist? This section answers all three 
  without marketing language.
